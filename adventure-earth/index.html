A brief introduction

Planet Earth is the third planet from the Sun in this solar system and is one of four terrestrial planets. Various flora and fauna inhabit this planet from large redwood trees to small microscopic organisms. One noticeable feature of this planet is how blue it is compared to its neighbors within the asteroid belt separating these terrestrial planets from the extra-terrestrial planets.

Landmasses are visible from space and are where land dwelling organisms live, which include humans. Humans are the beings that formed civilizations in which groups of them worked on feeding the population, some defended the settlements from threats, while a select few governed over these lands. Over time, countries where formed and cities expanded as the population of humankind grew exponentially.

In this adventure, you get to choose what you would like to do during your visit! This includes studying its geological features, visiting any continent to learn more about the countries and the flora and fauna that inhabit these regions, and more.
